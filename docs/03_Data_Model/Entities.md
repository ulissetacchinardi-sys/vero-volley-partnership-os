# 1. Purpose

This document defines every business entity that exists within the Vero Volley Partnership OS.

Entities represent the fundamental building blocks of the platform.

Every feature, workflow, report, automation and AI capability is built upon these entities.

This document is considered the authoritative reference for the Domain Model.

---

# 2. Domain Philosophy

The Partnership OS is not designed around spreadsheets.

It is designed around business objects.

Each entity represents a real-world concept used daily by the commercial department.

Examples include:

- a company
- a contact
- a sponsorship opportunity
- a commercial proposal
- an activity
- a match invitation

The system should model reality as naturally as possible.

---

# 3. Core Business Entities

The following entities form the foundation of the platform.

## User

Represents a member of the organization using the system.

Examples:

- Partnership Manager
- Commercial Director
- Marketing
- Administration

Responsibilities:

- owns records
- performs activities
- manages relationships

---

## Company

Represents an organization that may become, or already is, a commercial partner.

Examples:

- Hilti
- Numia
- Iren
- PhotoSì

A Company is the central business entity of the platform.

Every commercial relationship begins with a company.

---

## Contact

Represents a person working for a company.

Examples:

- Marketing Director
- CEO
- HR Manager
- Procurement Manager

A company may contain multiple contacts.

Every interaction happens with people, not companies.

---

## Opportunity

Represents a commercial negotiation.

Examples:

- New sponsorship
- Renewal
- Hospitality agreement
- Event partnership

An Opportunity progresses through the commercial pipeline.

---

## Activity

Represents every interaction between Vero Volley and an external stakeholder.

Examples:

- phone call
- meeting
- email
- WhatsApp
- business lunch
- match invitation
- follow-up

Activities create the commercial timeline.

---

# 4. Commercial Entities

These entities support the sales process.

## Proposal

Represents a sponsorship proposal.

A Proposal includes:

- version
- value
- status
- sent date
- linked PDF
- Google Drive document
- negotiation history

One Opportunity may contain multiple Proposal versions.

---

## Contract

Represents the commercial agreement.

Examples:

- annual partnership
- multi-year agreement
- VIK agreement

A Contract closes an Opportunity.

---

## Document

Represents every commercial document.

Examples:

- presentations
- PDFs
- images
- contracts
- spreadsheets

Documents are linked to Companies, Opportunities or Proposals.

---

# 5. Matchday Entities

Professional sport creates unique commercial assets.

These require dedicated entities.

---

## Match

Represents an official sporting event.

A Match includes:

- date
- venue
- competition
- opponent

Matches become commercial opportunities.

---

## Invitation

Represents an invitation sent to a guest.

Tracks:

- invited company
- invited contact
- confirmation
- attendance

---

## Hospitality

Represents the complete hospitality experience.

Includes:

- guests
- seats
- lounge
- business events
- networking

Hospitality is considered part of the commercial process.

# 9. Entity Principles

Every entity should eventually support:

- Unique Identifier
- Ownership
- Timeline
- Activities
- Documents
- AI Metadata

Whenever possible, entities should be reusable across multiple workflows.

# 12. Guiding Principles

Every entity should answer three questions.

## Why does this entity exist?

The entity must represent a real business concept.

---

## Who owns it?

Every business entity should have clear ownership.

---

## How does it create value?

The entity should contribute to commercial work.

If an entity does not simplify the work of the Partnership Team, its existence should be reconsidered.

---

# 13. Long-Term Vision

The Domain Model should evolve into a complete digital representation of the commercial operations of Vero Volley.

The objective is not simply to manage sponsorships.

The objective is to model every commercial relationship, every interaction and every opportunity inside one coherent business ecosystem.
