# Commercial User Journey

**Version:** 1.0  
**Status:** Approved  
**Document Owner:** Product Team  
**Last Updated:** July 2026

---

# 1. Purpose

This document describes how a Partnership Manager works throughout a typical working day and how the Vero Volley Partnership OS should support every activity.

The objective is not to describe software screens.

The objective is to describe the commercial workflow.

Every feature implemented in the platform should improve one or more phases of this workflow.

---

# 2. Guiding Principle

The CRM is not the destination.

It is the tool that helps the commercial team achieve better results.

Every interaction with the system should reduce administrative work and increase time spent building relationships.

---

# 3. Daily Workflow

A Partnership Manager's day revolves around five recurring activities:

- Planning
- Relationship Management
- Business Development
- Negotiation
- Follow-up

The software should continuously support these activities.

---

# 4. Beginning of the Day

The first screen opened every morning is the Dashboard.

The user should immediately understand:

- What requires attention today.
- Which meetings are scheduled.
- Which follow-ups are overdue.
- Which proposals require action.
- Which companies should be contacted.
- Upcoming matches available for hospitality invitations.

The Dashboard should answer one question:

**"What should I do today?"**

---

# 5. Managing Existing Relationships

Most of the working day is dedicated to maintaining existing relationships.

Typical activities include:

- Phone calls
- Emails
- Meetings
- WhatsApp conversations
- Business lunches
- Match invitations

Every interaction should be recorded as an Activity.

The commercial history should always remain visible.

---

# 6. Company Workspace

The Company Workspace is the operational center of every commercial relationship.

When opening a company, the user should immediately understand:

- Who the company is.
- Who the decision makers are.
- Current negotiations.
- Previous activities.
- Documents shared.
- Proposals sent.
- Hospitality history.
- Match invitations.
- Commercial opportunities.

The Workspace should answer one question:

**"What is the complete relationship with this company?"**

---

# 7. Contact Management

Contacts are people, not database records.

Every contact should display:

- personal information;
- role;
- communication history;
- meetings;
- related companies;
- related opportunities;
- activities.

The CRM should help users understand the relationship with the individual, not only the company.

---

# 8. Opportunity Management

Commercial opportunities represent potential partnerships.

Each opportunity should clearly display:

- current stage;
- estimated value;
- probability;
- expected closing date;
- related activities;
- proposals;
- stakeholders.

The objective is to understand:

**"What is needed to close this partnership?"**

---

# 9. Business Development

The commercial team continuously researches new companies.

The platform should support:

- Sponsor Research
- AI suggestions
- Lead generation
- Qualification
- Prioritization

The objective is to spend more time evaluating opportunities and less time searching for information.

---

# 10. Proposal Management

Sending a proposal is not the end of the process.

The CRM should support:

- proposal versions;
- proposal status;
- delivery date;
- linked documents;
- follow-up reminders;
- negotiation history.

Every proposal should remain connected to the opportunity and the company.

---

# 11. Match Invitations

Matches are one of the most valuable commercial assets.

The CRM should manage:

- invitations;
- accepted invitations;
- hospitality;
- guests;
- attendance history;
- relationship outcomes.

Hospitality is considered part of the commercial process.

---

# 12. End of the Day

Before finishing the working day, the user should quickly verify:

- completed activities;
- remaining follow-ups;
- updated opportunities;
- tomorrow's meetings.

The system should provide confidence that nothing important has been forgotten.

---

# 13. AI Assistant

The AI Assistant should act as a commercial copilot.

Examples:

- Suggest today's priorities.
- Summarise company history.
- Recommend follow-up actions.
- Identify inactive opportunities.
- Suggest potential sponsors.
- Highlight relationship risks.

The AI should always explain why a recommendation is being made.

---

# 14. Design Objectives

Every workflow should respect the following principles:

- Reduce clicks.
- Reduce repetitive work.
- Preserve commercial context.
- Never lose relationship history.
- Encourage proactive follow-up.
- Surface recommendations instead of requiring manual searches.

---

# 15. Success Criteria

The CRM is successful when a Partnership Manager can complete the majority of daily commercial activities without leaving the platform.

The objective is not simply to centralize information.

The objective is to increase commercial effectiveness.

---

# 16. Long-Term Vision

The Partnership OS should become the daily operating environment for the entire commercial department.

Every future feature should strengthen this vision.

Whenever a new capability is proposed, one question should always be asked:

**Does this improve the daily work of the Partnership Manager?**

If the answer is no, the feature should be reconsidered before implementation.
